Be Prepared

Range Rules & Safety

Safety is our #1 priority. All flyers must follow these rules.

Members need their current LUNAR membership card and a valid government-issued photo ID. High-power flyers must also bring their NAR certification card. Guests should bring $10 for the range fee. All flyers must sign the day's waiver before flying.
Our FAA waiver covers all motor classes from 1/4A through N (total impulse up to 40,960 N·s). Motors above G-class require a valid NAR high-power certification of the appropriate level. All experimental (EX) motors require prior approval from the Chief RSO.
All rockets must be inspected and approved by the Range Safety Officer before being placed on the pad. The launch area must be clear of non-flying personnel before ignition. No alcohol is permitted at the range. All participants must follow the RSO's instructions at all times. Failure to comply results in immediate removal from the range.
Rockets drifting outside the recovery boundary must be retrieved only after receiving clearance from the RSO. If a rocket lands on private property, contact a club officer — do not trespass. LUNAR has established relationships with neighboring landowners to assist with recovery.
Commercially manufactured APCP and black-powder motors are permitted. All motors must be LEUP-compliant. Members wishing to fly experimental motors must hold a valid LEUP and obtain RSO approval well in advance. Igniters are available for purchase at the range, or you may bring your own approved igniters.
