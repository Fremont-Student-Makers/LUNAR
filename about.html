Who We Are

About LUNAR

A passionate community of amateur rocketeers united by a love of flight, science, and the shared thrill of watching something you built disappear into the sky.

Our Mission

Rocketry Is for Everyone

LUNAR was founded on the belief that the sky is not a limit â€” it's a destination. We exist to make high-power amateur rocketry accessible, safe, and welcoming to anyone who wants to participate.

We are a chartered section of the National Association of Rocketry (NAR), the oldest and largest sport rocketry organization in the world. Membership in LUNAR automatically provides NAR membership benefits.

Our members range from curious newcomers building their first model kit to veteran flyers pushing the envelope of amateur rocketry with composite motors, on-board electronics, and dual-deployment recovery systems.

Our Story

Club History

From a handful of enthusiasts to a thriving community of 150+ members.

1999
Club Founded
A group of twelve rocketry enthusiasts chartered LUNAR as an official NAR section and held their first launch at a local farm.
2003
First High-Power Waiver
LUNAR secured its first FAA Certificate of Waiver, allowing members to fly motors up to 40,960 NÂ·s total impulse.
2008
Permanent Launch Site
Partnership with a local landowner gave LUNAR a dedicated, year-round launch site at Montezuma Hills.
2013
STEM Outreach Program
Launched a formal youth education program, bringing rocketry demonstrations to over 20 local schools annually.
2018
100-Member Milestone
Club membership surpassed 100 active members for the first time, reflecting growing interest in amateur rocketry.
2024
Silver Anniversary Celebration
Celebrated 25 years of launches with a special two-day event featuring club members, alumni, and a record-breaking altitude attempt.
